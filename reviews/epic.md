# Accessibility Review: EPIC Museum

**Observer:** Geo Johnson & Kelston Richmond 

**Date:** 2026-05-14  
**Overall compliance:** 55% of rated items met  
**Summary:** 26 met, 9 not met, 12 partial, 0 N/A  
**Total rated items:** 47

---

## Exhibits & Objects

- ✅ Small objects mounted at or below 40 in. (seated eye level) — **Met**
- ✅ Display cases shallow enough to see objects from above — **Met**
- ⚠ Objects have 70% color contrast against their background — **Partial**
- ⚠ Touchable objects or reproductions available for tactile access — **Partial**
  > There are objects that you can touch, but there is no braille for people with sight disabilities to read.
- ⚠ Audio description provided for items that cannot be touched — **Partial**
  > They have headphones at the beginning of the museum, but they don't really announce them to people. They also use screens that you need to press to operate. I didn't use one, so I don't know if they speak to you prior to you knowing what you're pressing, such as with a screen reader.
- ⚠ No objects protrude more than 4 in. from walls at head height — **Partial**
  > This varies. An object did graze my hat, but I don't think many other people would have that issue. Some objects are higher than others and don't affect you as much.
- ⚠ No low floor-level objects that could be tripping hazards — **Partial**
  > There are low rising bars that might prevent you from walking into something, but they could be just as dangerous as the object on the ground.
- ✅ At least 100 lux of light on displayed objects — **Met**

---

## Labels & Text

- ⚠ Labels use plain English — short sentences, active voice — **Partial**
  > Short English sentences, but no voice reading them.
- ✅ Legible font used — no all-caps or decorative scripts — **Met**
- ✅ Type size appropriate for the viewing distance — **Met**
- ✅ High contrast between label text and background (70%) — **Met**
- ✅ Labels printed on matte, non-glare surfaces — **Met**
- ⚠ Wall labels centered at approx. 54 in. (readable seated & standing) — **Partial**
  > Might vary a few inches, so take this with a grain of salt.
- ✅ Label positions consistent throughout the exhibition — **Met**
- ⚠ Alternative formats available: Braille, large print, or audio guide — **Partial**
  > Only audio. Refer to prior notes.
- ⚠ 100–300 lux of light on all label surfaces — **Partial**
  > Some rooms were much darker than others.

---

## Audio/Visual & Interactives

- ✅ All videos and audio programs are captioned or have printed scripts — **Met**
- ✅ Silent audiovisuals labelled to confirm no audio is being missed — **Met**
- ✅ Audio description available for image-based content — **Met**
- ✅ Interactive controls reachable from a wheelchair (max. 48 in. height) — **Met**
- ✅ Controls usable one-handed — no tight grip or twisting required — **Met**
- ❌ Touch targets at least 3 in. in diameter — **Not met**
  > Might be one inch in diameter.
- ❌ Instructions provided in both print and audio formats — **Not met**
  > I actually didn't see many instructions. I think they think the screens and touchable things are intuitive.
- ✅ No furniture or objects blocking access to interactives — **Met**
- ⚠ Assistive listening system available in programming spaces — **Partial**
  > Only if you opt in at the beginning of the museum.

---

## Pathways & Navigation

- ✅ Main circulation route at least 36 in. wide (60 in. preferred) — **Met**
- ✅ At least 80 in. of clear overhead clearance throughout — **Met**
- ✅ Route is step-free, or ramps/lifts provided at level changes — **Met**
- ❌ Floor surface is stable, firm, and slip-resistant — **Not met**
- ❌ Route well lit (50–100 lux) with no confusing shadow pools — **Not met**
- ❌ 70% color contrast between floor and walls/furniture — **Not met**
- ❌ Accessible floor map available at the gallery entrance — **Not met**
- ✅ More than one exit from the exhibition — **Met**
- ✅ 60 in. wheelchair turning space at any dead ends — **Met**

---

## Seating & Furniture

- ❌ Seating provided in every gallery (or immediately adjacent) — **Not met**
- ❌ At least 50% of seats are accessible — **Not met**
- ❌ Seats have both back and arm support; seat height 17–19 in. — **Not met**
- ✅ Space for wheelchair users beside fixed seating — **Met**
- ✅ Seats clearly visible — contrast with floors and walls — **Met**
- ⚠ Seats do not block pathways or interactive controls — **Partial**
- ✅ Display cases have cane-detectable lower edges (at or below 27 in.) — **Met**
