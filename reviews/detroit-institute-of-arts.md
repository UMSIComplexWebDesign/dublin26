# Accessibility review: Detroit Institute of Arts

**Observer:** Jane Smith  
**Date:** 2026-05-03  
**Overall compliance:** 82% of rated items met (32 met, 4 not met, 3 partial, 0 N/A)

## Exhibits & objects

- ✅ Small objects mounted at or below 40 in. (seated eye level) — **Met**
- ✅ Display cases shallow enough to see objects from above — **Met**
- ✅ Objects have 70% color contrast against their background — **Met**
- ❌ Touchable objects or reproductions available for tactile access — **Not met**
  > No tactile alternatives offered anywhere in the main galleries.
- ⚠️ Audio description provided for items that cannot be touched — **Partial**
  > Available via the museum app, but signage directing visitors to it was hard to find.
- ✅ No objects protrude more than 4 in. from walls at head height — **Met**
- ✅ No low floor-level objects that could be tripping hazards — **Met**
- ✅ At least 100 lux of light on displayed objects — **Met**

## Labels & text

- ✅ Labels use plain English — short sentences, active voice — **Met**
- ✅ Legible font used — no all-caps or decorative scripts — **Met**
- ✅ Type size appropriate for the viewing distance — **Met**
- ✅ High contrast between label text and background (70%) — **Met**
- ✅ Labels printed on matte, non-glare surfaces — **Met**
- ✅ Wall labels centered at approx. 54 in. (readable seated & standing) — **Met**
- ✅ Label positions consistent throughout the exhibition — **Met**
- ➖ Alternative formats available: Braille, large print, or audio guide — **N/A**
  > Not assessed during this visit.
- ✅ 100–300 lux of light on all label surfaces — **Met**

## Audio/visual & interactives

- ✅ All videos and audio programs are captioned or have printed scripts — **Met**
- ✅ Silent audiovisuals labelled to confirm no audio is being missed — **Met**
- ⚠️ Audio description available for image-based content — **Partial**
- ✅ Interactive controls reachable from a wheelchair (max. 48 in. height) — **Met**
- ✅ Controls usable one-handed — no tight grip or twisting required — **Met**
- ✅ Touch targets at least 3 in. in diameter — **Met**
- ✅ Instructions provided in both print and audio formats — **Met**
- ❌ No furniture or objects blocking access to interactives — **Not met**
  > Two interactive stations in the modern wing had benches placed directly in front of them.
- ✅ Assistive listening system available in programming spaces — **Met**

## Pathways & navigation

- ✅ Main circulation route at least 36 in. wide (60 in. preferred) — **Met**
- ✅ At least 80 in. of clear overhead clearance throughout — **Met**
- ✅ Route is step-free, or ramps/lifts provided at level changes — **Met**
- ✅ Floor surface is stable, firm, and slip-resistant — **Met**
- ✅ Route well lit (50–100 lux) with no confusing shadow pools — **Met**
- ✅ 70% color contrast between floor and walls/furniture — **Met**
- ❌ Accessible floor map available at the gallery entrance — **Not met**
  > No floor map at any of the three entrances we used.
- ✅ More than one exit from the exhibition — **Met**
- ✅ 60 in. wheelchair turning space at any dead ends — **Met**

## Seating & furniture

- ✅ Seating provided in every gallery (or immediately adjacent) — **Met**
- ✅ At least 50% of seats are accessible — **Met**
- ✅ Seats have both back and arm support; seat height 17–19 in. — **Met**
- ✅ Space for wheelchair users beside fixed seating — **Met**
- ✅ Seats clearly visible — contrast with floors and walls — **Met**
- ✅ Seats do not block pathways or interactive controls — **Met**
- ❌ Display cases have cane-detectable lower edges (at or below 27 in.) — **Not met**
  > Several free-standing cases in the ancient world gallery had no cane-detectable base.

## Safety & content

- ✅ Both visual and audible fire alarms are present — **Met**
- ✅ Accessible emergency exits clearly marked with access symbols — **Met**
- ✅ Exhibition addresses multiple intellectual levels and sensory channels — **Met**
- ✅ People with disabilities represented respectfully in exhibit content — **Met**
- ✅ Labels use person-first language throughout — **Met**
