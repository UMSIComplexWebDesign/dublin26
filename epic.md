# Accessibility review: Epic Museum

**Observer:** Colleen van Lent  
**Date:** 2026-05-06  
**Overall compliance:** 13% of rated items met (6 met, 30 not met, 11 partial, 0 N/A)

## Exhibits & objects

- ✅ Small objects mounted at or below 40 in. (seated eye level) — **Met**
- ❌ Display cases shallow enough to see objects from above — **Not met**
- ⚠️ Objects have 70% color contrast against their background — **Partial**
- ❌ Touchable objects or reproductions available for tactile access — **Not met**
- ⚠️ Audio description provided for items that cannot be touched — **Partial**
- ❌ No objects protrude more than 4 in. from walls at head height — **Not met**
- ❌ No low floor-level objects that could be tripping hazards — **Not met**
- ⚠️ At least 100 lux of light on displayed objects — **Partial**

## Labels & text

- ❌ Labels use plain English — short sentences, active voice — **Not met**
- ⚠️ Legible font used — no all-caps or decorative scripts — **Partial**
- ❌ Type size appropriate for the viewing distance — **Not met**
- ❌ High contrast between label text and background (70%) — **Not met**
- ✅ Labels printed on matte, non-glare surfaces — **Met**
- ❌ Wall labels centered at approx. 54 in. (readable seated & standing) — **Not met**
- ❌ Label positions consistent throughout the exhibition — **Not met**
- ❌ Alternative formats available: Braille, large print, or audio guide — **Not met**
- ❌ 100–300 lux of light on all label surfaces — **Not met**

## Audio/visual & interactives

- ❌ All videos and audio programs are captioned or have printed scripts — **Not met**
- ⚠️ Silent audiovisuals labelled to confirm no audio is being missed — **Partial**
- ❌ Audio description available for image-based content — **Not met**
- ❌ Interactive controls reachable from a wheelchair (max. 48 in. height) — **Not met**
- ❌ Controls usable one-handed — no tight grip or twisting required — **Not met**
- ❌ Touch targets at least 3 in. in diameter — **Not met**
- ⚠️ Instructions provided in both print and audio formats — **Partial**
- ⚠️ No furniture or objects blocking access to interactives — **Partial**
- ❌ Assistive listening system available in programming spaces — **Not met**

## Pathways & navigation

- ❌ Main circulation route at least 36 in. wide (60 in. preferred) — **Not met**
- ❌ At least 80 in. of clear overhead clearance throughout — **Not met**
- ❌ Route is step-free, or ramps/lifts provided at level changes — **Not met**
- ❌ Floor surface is stable, firm, and slip-resistant — **Not met**
- ❌ Route well lit (50–100 lux) with no confusing shadow pools — **Not met**
- ❌ 70% color contrast between floor and walls/furniture — **Not met**
- ⚠️ Accessible floor map available at the gallery entrance — **Partial**
- ⚠️ More than one exit from the exhibition — **Partial**
- ⚠️ 60 in. wheelchair turning space at any dead ends — **Partial**

## Seating & furniture

- ❌ Seating provided in every gallery (or immediately adjacent) — **Not met**
- ❌ At least 50% of seats are accessible — **Not met**
- ❌ Seats have both back and arm support; seat height 17–19 in. — **Not met**
- ❌ Space for wheelchair users beside fixed seating — **Not met**
- ⚠️ Seats clearly visible — contrast with floors and walls — **Partial**
- ❌ Seats do not block pathways or interactive controls — **Not met**
- ✅ Display cases have cane-detectable lower edges (at or below 27 in.) — **Met**

## Safety & content

- ✅ Both visual and audible fire alarms are present — **Met**
- ✅ Accessible emergency exits clearly marked with access symbols — **Met**
- ❌ Exhibition addresses multiple intellectual levels and sensory channels — **Not met**
- ❌ People with disabilities represented respectfully in exhibit content — **Not met**
- ✅ Labels use person-first language throughout — **Met**

*Checklist based on the [Smithsonian Guidelines for Accessible Exhibition Design](https://www.sifacilities.si.edu/sites/default/files/Files/Accessibility/accessible-exhibition-design1.pdf)*
